## What is IEC 60079?

IEC 60079 is the international standard series that covers electrical equipment used in **explosive atmospheres** — the kind of environments we deal with every day on oil & gas sites.

As E&I QC Engineers, this standard is one of the most important we need to understand. A wrong equipment selection or a poorly sealed cable gland in a Zone 1 area can have catastrophic consequences.

---

## Hazardous Area Zones

The standard defines zones based on the **likelihood of an explosive atmosphere being present**:

| Zone | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Zone 0 | Explosive atmosphere present **continuously** |
| Zone 1 | Likely to occur in **normal operation** |
| Zone 2 | Not likely in normal operation, but possible |

For dust atmospheres, the equivalent zones are Zone 20, 21, and 22.

---

## Equipment Categories

Equipment is marked with a **Group** and **Category**:

- **Group I** — Mining applications
- **Group II** — Surface industries (our world — oil & gas, petrochemical)

Categories for Group II:
- **Category 1G** — Suitable for Zone 0, 1, 2
- **Category 2G** — Suitable for Zone 1, 2
- **Category 3G** — Suitable for Zone 2 only

---

## Key Lesson from the Field

During inspections, always verify:
1. The equipment **Ex marking** matches the area classification drawing
2. The **certificate number** on the nameplate is valid and not expired
3. Cable glands are **certified and correctly installed**
4. No unauthorised modifications have been made

> "If it's not certified and documented, it doesn't belong in a hazardous area."
